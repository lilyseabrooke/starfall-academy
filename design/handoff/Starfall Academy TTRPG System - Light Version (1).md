# Starfall Academy

---

So, because you have good taste, you’re going to try out the Starfall Academy system. Smart choice.

Starfall is a system that draws heavily on intrigue and mystery, with a modern-day magic-academy setting, cinematic action, and a character-forward focus with a rich mechanical system that serves mystery, investigation, roleplaying and character development.

We’ll start this guide with an introduction to character creation, and then below that will be the quick and dirty guide to how the game is actually played. So bring your wands, double-check your potions, and don’t get into trouble.  
Who am I kidding? Of course you’re going to get into trouble.

---

# Character Creation

When creating a character, you’ll want to pick a few things to help define them, starting with, you know, a name. That’s pretty important.

Every student needs to have a **Subject** they study, what field of magic they’re in. There are 24 fields of magic, 4 groups of 6, listed in the [Fields of Magic](#fields-of-magic) section below.  
Then you’ll want to either pick one Class to start with at rank 4, or two Classes to start with at rank 2 each. The classes are listed in the [Classes](#classes) section below.  
Then you’ll pick one of the three starting Wands for your character, which are listed in the [Wands](#wands) section below.  
And then you’ll allocate your starting points, which is elaborated on in the [Stats](#stats) section below.  
Fill out the rest of your inventory, including any special bonuses gained from your starting points, in the [Inventory](#inventory) section below.  
Finally, you can pick your spells, which is elaborated on in the [Spells](#spells) section below.

## Fields of Magic {#fields-of-magic}

| Creation Magics | Natural Magics | Spectral Magics | Wisdom Magics |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| Alchemy Artificy Enchantment Herbalism Runology Wandcrafting | Evocation Illusion Restoration Telekinesis Transmutation Warding | Divination Chronomancy Hypnomancy Necromancy Summoning Teleportation | Arcane History Bestiology Counterhexology Crystallomancy Demonology Draconology |

A student will typically choose one **Subject** as their major, but some ambitious ones will pick two. (It is technically possible to pick three or even four, but that’s getting into nitty gritty details and you should probably stick to two at maximum.)  
If you want detailed descriptions of the fields and examples of spells in each one, pop down to the [Spells section](?tab=t.4gj5qw5jrssj). There’s more than you know.

## Classes {#classes}

You can have as many Classes as you like, but to start with, you can only pick one or two. If you pick one, you get to start at rank 4, while if you pick two, you have to start at rank 2 in each.  
There are twelve Classes, each with ten possible ranks. For each rank, you get to pick between two abilities, which lets you customize your character’s traits and lean into what kind of class archetype you want to play. [You can find the class list in all its detail here](?tab=t.5np415oy63ma), but for quick reference, the classes are in the table below.

| Pupil | Socialite | Rascal | Heir |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| An expert in studying magic and learning spells | A natural charmer who resolves situations with words | A skilled liar and sneak who can get in and out of anywhere | A person gifted with magical power and/or authority |
| **Renegade** | **Naturalist** | **Alchemist** | **Professor** |
| A person who doesn’t fit into society, sticking to strange creatures and other rebels | A master of the magics of the natural world, whether mundane or arcane | An expert in brewing and using magical potions | An authority figure with expertise and responsibility |
| **Enforcer** | **Artificer** | **Wandjock** | **Mastermind** |
| An authority figure tasked with upholding justice and order | An expert in the use and maintenance of powerful magic artifacts | A spellcasting genius with a flair for the dramatic | A strategist who gets their way without ever getting directly involved |

## Wands {#wands}

When you create your character, you will pick from 3 starting wands:

| Sylene’s Crystal | Champion’s Wand | Whispered Secrets |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| Gives \+2 to any one Stat, chosen at creation. | Gives \+2 to any three Abilities (Subjects or Skills), chosen at creation. | Gives \+4 to any one Ability (Subject or Skill), chosen at creation. |

You will be able to acquire more wands through gameplay, and you can see all the possible wand options in the [Wands section](?tab=t.wi2kwf4we33e).

## Stats {#stats}

Now, you can allocate your starting points and decide the strengths and weaknesses of your character.  
You have 3 categories you can develop Ranks in: Stats, Subjects, and Skills.  
Your **Stats** determine your base abilities. Your Stats inform your ability to use magic, physical and mental skills, and resist conditions. The stats and what they’re tied to are described in the table here:

| Stats |  |  |  |  |  |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Focus** |  | **Creativity** |  | **Logic** |  |
| **Subjects** | **Skills** | **Subjects** | **Skills** | **Subjects** | **Skills** |
| **Wandcrafting Evocation Telekinesis Hypnomancy Teleportation Arcane History** | **Concentration Recall Information Search Willpower** | **Alchemy Artificy Enchantment Illusion Transmutation Summoning** | **Art Hide Object Improvise Sleight of Hand** | **Runology Warding Necromancy Counterhexology Crystallomancy Demonology** | **Analyze Comprehend Research Tracking** |
| **Resists Doubt** |  | **Resists Loss** |  | **Resists Fear** |  |
| **Insight** |  | **Body** |  | **Charm** |  |
| **Subjects** | **Skills** | **Subjects** | **Skills** | **Subjects** | **Skills** |
| **Herbalism Restoration Chronomancy Divination Bestiology Draconology** | **Creature Investigate Perception Read Person** | **None** | **Agility Athletics Endurance Stealth** | **None** | **Deception Persuasion Tact Win Over** |
| **Resists Despair** |  | **Resists Wound** |  | **Does not resist any Conditions** |  |

Your typical **Quick Build** gives you **10** points in Stats, **15** in Subjects, and **15** in Skills, with a limit of **5** on each Stat, Subject, or Skill. However, that’s assuming a first-year student in a standard game—you get more points if you play a higher-year character.  
In general, you’ll stick with a first-year character. More experienced players might prefer to pick up a second, third, or fourth-year, or even a character who’s graduated, in order to try out more advanced builds.  
Discuss with your GM what year your character is in. In general, a party should all be on the same page, so that nobody is overshadowing the lower-year characters, but you can play with differences in years. Your GM might give a benefit to a lower-year character, like a powerful artifact or influential status on campus, or a handicap to a higher-year character, like a curse or a bad reputation.

You can also choose to fine-tune your character with a **Custom Build** instead of the **Quick Build.**  
The two options are listed below.

### Quick Build

For the **Quick Build,** you have:

1. **10 ranks** for your Stats  
2. **15 ranks** for your Subjects  
3. **15 ranks** for your Skills

You cannot increase any one Stat, Subject, or Skill above 5 at character creation.

For higher years, the number of ranks you get for Stats, Subjects, and Skills each increase, along with the individual limit you can put on each one.

| Quick Build at Higher Years |  |  |  |  |
| :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Year** | **Stat Ranks** | **Subject Ranks** | **Skill Ranks** | **Limit** |
| **First** | **10** | **15** | **15** | **5** |
| **Second** | **15** | **25** | **25** | **6** |
| **Third** | **20** | **35** | **35** | **7** |
| **Fourth** | **25** | **45** | **45** | **8** |
| **Graduate** | **30** | **55** | **55** | **9** |

### Custom Build

For the **Custom Build,** you instead have a total pool of points, and you can allocate them however you like. Increasing your **Stat** ranks costs 3 points for each, while increasing your **Ability** ranks (the combined term for **Subjects** or **Skills**) costs 1 point for each. As before, each individual Stat or Ability is limited to 5\.  
For a higher-year character, you get more points and a higher limit, as below.

| Custom Build at Higher Years |  |  |
| :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Year** | **Points** | **Limit** |
| **First** | **60** | **5** |
| **Second** | **90** | **6** |
| **Third** | **120** | **7** |
| **Fourth** | **150** | **8** |
| **Graduate** | **180** | **9** |

When you take the **Custom Build** option, you can also choose to spend some of your points on other advantages:

1. **Class Ranks:** You can spend points to increase your class rank above the default 4 or 2/2, **2 points per rank level** (e.g. 4 points for rank 2, 10 points for rank 5\)**.**  
2. **Wands:** You can spend points to purchase a new wand, 1 point for every 400 material cost of the wand.  
3. **Artifacts:** You can spend points to purchase an artifact, 1 point for every 400 material cost of the artifact. You will already be attuned to any artifacts you take at character creation.

(After this, if you’re using the handy-dandy automatic character sheet, you’ll have to set your Rank Points to zero, because they automatically update to track your Ability rank increases, but the points from character creation don’t count. You can just select the number value and change it.)

## Inventory {#inventory}

At character creation, take 500 Materials, which represents your general spellcasting and crafting materials, as well as an impromptu trading currency.

You can take more in your inventory depending on your magic allocation.

1. **Alchemy:** For each point you put in Alchemy, you can take one [potion](#heading=h.zc1igj9r3gu0), up to a maximum of 6\. You know the recipe for each potion you take this way.  
2. **Runology:** For each point you put in Runology, you learn two [glyphs](#heading=h.kq8e0onjnwcv).  
3. **Wandcrafting:** For each point you put in Wandcrafting, you can start with an extra [wand](#heading=h.b15cpp61prqw) worth 200 materials. These can be pooled: for example, with 5 points in Wandcrafting, you can start with a 1000-material wand, a 600-material wand and a 400-material wand, or whatever else makes you happy. If you put points in Wandcrafting, you will have access to a wandcrafting studio with a few other wandcrafters, referred to as your studiomates. Work out with the GM what your studio is like and how you get along with your studiomates.

## Spells {#spells}

Finally, you can get to the fun part—selecting your character’s spells\! You’ll want to pick from [the Spells list here](?tab=t.4gj5qw5jrssj).

As a first-year student, you can take any **5 Basic spells, 10 Standard spells, and 1 Advanced spell.** There’s no limits on what fields you can take these spells in, but you may find Standard spells difficult to cast outside of your main fields, to say nothing of Advanced spells.  
You can take more spells at higher years:

| **Third** | **10** | **15** | **2** |
